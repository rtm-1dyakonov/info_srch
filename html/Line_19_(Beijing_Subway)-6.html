Line 19 of the Beijing Subway (Chinese: 北京地铁19号线; pinyin: Běijīng Dìtiě shíjiǔ hàoxiàn) is a rapid transit line in Beijing. It is 22.4 km (13.9 mi) in length and has 10 stations (6 stations in operation). It is fully underground.[2]

Description[edit]

Phase 1 of Line 19 begins at Mudanyuan station in Haidian District and ends at Xingong station in Fengtai District.[4] The line is 22.4 km (13.9 mi) in length with 10 stations of which 4 are still under construction.[5] The line was envisioned as a major relief line for the overcrowded Line 4 and more direct link to the Beijing Financial Street commercial area.

The line uses 8-car type A rolling stock.[3][6]

The trains have a maximum speed of 120 km/h. However the speed limit of the first phase, which runs under the dense city center is 100 km/h.[7][8] Phase II extensions north and south run through suburban areas and will be built to more generous alignments allowing for 120km/h operations.

History[edit]

It was reported in February 2012 as one of six new lines under planning by the city's public transit planning authorities.[citation needed] As of 2015, the line was renamed to Line 19[13] with the first phase starting construction in 2016.[4] The line opened in 31 December 2021, but 4 stations is still unopened.[14][15]

Future development[edit]

Phase 2 of Line 19, which is under planning, consists of a northern extension to Changping District. Additionally, there will be a branch line of the northern extension, which will serve the rebuilt Qinghe railway station, a station on the Beijing–Zhangjiakou intercity railway.[16] On 11 January 2022, the second phase of Line 19 was listed as one of the 10 construction projects in the "Beijing Rail Transit Phase III Construction Plan (2022-2027)"[17]
