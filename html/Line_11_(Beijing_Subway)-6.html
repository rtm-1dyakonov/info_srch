Line 11 of the Beijing Subway (Chinese: 北京地铁11号线; pinyin: běijīng dìtiě shíyī hàoxiàn) is a rapid transit line of the Beijing Subway.[7] The line opened on December 31, 2021.[3]

History[edit]

Old planning[edit]

In 2006, Line 11 was originally designed as an L-shaped line from Wanliu to Dahongmen, which together with the L-shaped Line 10, form a subway ring route.[10] After Line 10 Phase II took over the entire ring route, Line 11 was displaced.

In January 2010, the government of Shijingshan District disclosed plans for a Line 11 in western Beijing that would traverse the Shougang (Beijing Capital Steel) complex and intersect with Lines 4 and 16.[11][12]

Phase 1[edit]

Construction started on November 13, 2019.[7] The western section (Phase 1) of Line 11 (Moshikou station — Xinshougang station), also known as "Winter Olympic Branch Line", is 4.2 km in length with 4 stations.[4][5] It is fully underground.[13][14]

Three stations (Jin'anqiao, Beixin'an and Xinshougang (Shougang Park) stations) opened on December 31, 2021. Moshikou station will open in late 2022.

Phase 2[edit]

According to the information released on January 11, 2022, Phase II of Line 11 to Lize Shangwuqu and Yangqiao is included in the "Beijing Rail Transit Phase III Construction Plan".[15]
